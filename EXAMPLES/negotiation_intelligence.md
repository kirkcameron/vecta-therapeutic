## Example 1: The Delayed Response

### Input
```
"They said they need to 'think about it' but it's been two weeks."
```

### Vecta Analysis

#### Pattern Detected
**Delay → Possible power play**

#### Primary Interpretation: Stalling Tactic
- **Confidence:** HIGH (80%)
- **Pattern:** "Think about it" = delay without commitment
- **Signals:** STALLING, DELAY, PASSIVE_REFUSAL, AVOIDANCE
- **Meaning:** They're saying no without saying no
- **Framework:** Negotiation / Power Dynamics

#### Secondary: Genuine Hesitation
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM (35%)
- **Signals:** GENUINE_UNCERTAINTY, CONSIDERATION, WEIGHING
- **Meaning:** They haven't decided yet
- **Requires verification:** "Have they reached out with questions?"

#### Secondary: Internal blockers
- **Confidence:** LOW (25%)
- **Signals:** BUREAUCRACY, APPROVAL_NEEDED, CHAIN_OF_COMMAND
- **Meaning:** Someone else needs to approve

#### Excluded Interpretations
- ❌ NOT simply forgetful
- ❌ NOT benign

### What This Means

**Before assuming bad faith, consider:**
- Is this their decision or do they need approval?
- Have they reached out with questions?
- Is there a deadline they haven't shared?

**If it's a power play:**
- They may be waiting for you to sweeten the deal
- Consider: What are they really waiting for?

**If it's genuine:**
- Give them space to decide
- A gentle follow-up can help

### What to Do Next

1. **Assess context:** Do they have authority to decide alone?
2. **Follow up thoughtfully:** "I wanted to check in — do you have any questions?"
3. **Set boundaries:** "I need an answer by [date] to hold this offer."
4. **Read the signals:** If they go silent, assume disinterest.

## Example 2: The Quick Acceptance

### Input
```
"They accepted our offer immediately. Did we leave money on the table?"
```

### Vecta Analysis

#### Pattern Detected
**Rapid acceptance → Regret potential**

#### Primary Interpretation: Too Easy
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM-HIGH (70%)
- **Pattern:** Quick yes = potential undervaluing
- **Signals:** IMMEDIATE_ACCEPTANCE, EAGER, ENTHUSIASTIC, NO_NEGOTIATION
- **Meaning:** They may have been hoping you'd go higher
- **Framework:** Negotiation / Value Discovery

#### Secondary: Genuine Enthusiasm
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM (45%)
- **Signals:** FIT, ALIGNMENT, RELIEF, READY_TO_COMMIT
- **Meaning:** This was exactly what they wanted
- **Requires verification:** "Do you feel good about the deal?"

#### Secondary: Strategic
- **Confidence:** LOW (20%)
- **Signals:** PRETEND_EAGER, OVERAGREEING, FUTURE_LEVERAGE
- **Meaning:** They're setting up to ask for more later

#### Excluded Interpretations
- ❌ NOT a mistake (necessarily)

### What This Means

**The question isn't "did we leave money on the table" — it's:**
- "Did we understand their true value?"
- "Will they feel good about this deal long-term?"
- "Is this sustainable?"

**Quick acceptance can mean:**
1. They were going to accept anything
2. You underpriced
3. You perfectly matched their needs

### What to Do Next

1. **Post-negotiation check-in:** "How are you feeling about the terms?"
2. **Monitor for renegotiation:** Quick acceptors sometimes ask for more later
3. **Build the relationship:** Long-term value > short-term wins
4. **Don't second-guess:** You negotiated, they accepted. That's a deal.

## Example 3: The Ambiguous Response

### Input
```
"They said 'we'll circle back' but I don't know what that means."
```

### Vecta Analysis

#### Pattern Detected
**Ambiguity → Avoidance of commitment**

#### Primary Interpretation: Polite Refusal
- **Confidence:** HIGH (75%)
- **Pattern:** "Circle back" = corporate speak for "no"
- **Signals:** AMBIGUITY, AVOIDANCE, PASSIVE_DELAY, CORPORATE_SPEAK
- **Meaning:** They're not saying yes or no
- **Framework:** Negotiation / Power Dynamics

#### Secondary: Genuine Confusion
- **Confidence:** LOW (30%)
- **Signals:** UNCERTAINTY, NEEDS_TIME, CLARITY_REQUIRED
- **Meaning:** They genuinely don't know yet

#### Secondary: Schedule Conflict
- **Confidence:** LOW (25%)
- **Signals:** TIMING, LOGISTICS, NEXT_STEPS_UNCLEAR
- **Meaning:** It's not a "no," it's a "not now"

#### Excluded Interpretations
- ❌ NOT a typo
- ❌ NOT a promise

### What This Means

**"Circle back" is negotiation speak for:**
- "I'm not comfortable saying no directly"
- "I need more time to decide"
- "This isn't my priority right now"
- "Maybe" without the accountability

### What to Do Next

1. **Ask for clarity:** "What would 'circling back' look like? What's the timeline?"
2. **Set expectations:** "I need an answer by [date] — does that work?"
3. **Read the subtext:** If they deflect again, assume disinterest.
4. **Don't wait passively:** Follow up, but don't chase.

## Example 4: The Counter-Offer Dance

### Input
```
"They countered our proposal with terms we didn't expect. Where did this come from?"
```

### Vecta Analysis

#### Pattern Detected
**Unexpected counter → Hidden priorities**

#### Primary Interpretation: Revealed Preferences
- **Confidence:** HIGH (80%)
- **Pattern:** Unexpected counter = core issue revealed
- **Signals:** COUNTER, NEGOTIATION, REVEALED_PRIORITIES, HIDDEN_CONCERNS
- **Meaning:** They've shown you what they really care about
- **Framework:** Interest-Based Negotiation

#### Secondary: Testing
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM (35%)
- **Signals:** PROBING, BOUNDARY_TESTING, MAXIMIZING
- **Meaning:** They're seeing how far they can push

#### Secondary: Advisor Influence
- **Confidence:** LOW (25%)
- **Signals:** EXTERNAL_ADVISOR, LAWYER, CONSULTANT
- **Meaning:** Someone else is driving their position

#### Excluded Interpretations
- ❌ NOT random
- ❌ NOT an insult

### What This Means

**An unexpected counter is VALUABLE:**
- It reveals their real priorities
- It shows what they're willing to negotiate on
- It's an opening, not a rejection

### What to Do Next

1. **Analyze the counter:** What does it reveal about their priorities?
2. **Don't take it personally:** This is negotiation, not rejection.
3. **Respond strategically:** "I'm glad you brought this up — can you tell me more about why this matters to you?"
4. **Look for trade-offs:** If they want X, what are they willing to give for it?

## Example 5: The Silence After a Proposal

### Input
```
"We sent our proposal and heard nothing for a week. Then they sent a complaint about something small."
```

### Vecta Analysis

#### Pattern Detected
**Silence → Negative signal amplified**

#### Primary Interpretation: Processing / Resistance
- **Confidence:** HIGH (75%)
- **Pattern:** Silence after proposal + complaint = rejection likely
- **Signals:** SILENCE, DELAY, COMPLAINT, DEFLECTION, CRITICISM
- **Meaning:** They're building a case against the deal
- **Framework:** Negotiation / Conflict Signals

#### Secondary: Genuine Issue
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM (30%)
- **Signals:** REAL_CONCERN, VALID_POINT, PROCESSING
- **Meaning:** There's a legitimate issue they need addressed

#### Secondary: Power Play
- **Confidence:** LOW (25%)
- **Signals:** LEVERAGE, BARGAINING_POWER, PRESSURE
- **Meaning:** They're creating distance to gain advantage

#### Excluded Interpretations
- ❌ NOT benign
- ❌ NOT random complaint

### What This Means

**A complaint after silence is a RED FLAG:**
- They may be looking for an exit
- They may be testing your response
- They may be preparing to counter or reject

### What to Do Next

1. **Don't ignore the complaint:** Even if it's small, address it.
2. **Ask directly:** "I noticed we haven't heard back — is there something that needs to change?"
3. **Prepare for rejection:** If the silence continues, assume the deal is off.
4. **Don't overcompensate:** Responding to every complaint signals weakness.
